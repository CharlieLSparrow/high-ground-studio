# Quipsly & QuipLore Brand and Lore Guide

This document defines the core identity, lore, aesthetic, and product direction for the Quipsly and QuipLore ecosystem.

## 1. Core Identity & Structure

Some of it is outdated, particularly now Quipsly is a full blown SAAS platform now, or hopes to be. Quipsly began as the mascot name and has grown into a broader identity system. Quipsly may mean:
- the main sparrow mascot
- a species/family of little quote-keeping birds
- potentially Quipsly.com, a worldbuilding/brand/mascot companion site

**The current likely structure:**
- **QuipLore.com** = the quote platform, quote engine, archive, discovery app
- **Quipsly.com** = the whimsical village/world of the Quipsly, lore, merch, downloads, mascot content, style guides, maybe kid/family-friendly magic
- **Quipsly** = singular and plural preferred over “Quipslies” (e.g., “A Quipsly brought me a quote,” and “The Quipsly collect meaningful words.”)

### Core brand feeling

The emotional center is:
**Tiny meaningful fragments of humanity, gathered and shared by adorable little keeper-birds.**

- **QuipLore** should feel like a cozy, magical, authoritative quote archive with strong citation rigor under a warm storybook surface.
- **Quipsly** should feel like a “golden retriever librarian”: curious, loyal, emotionally sincere, a little chaotic around good sentences, never cynical or pushy.

### Defining words and phrases

**Strong brand words:**
warm, curious, cozy, literary, sincere, whimsical, trustworthy, tiny keeper of meaning, nest-building, collected wisdom, human wisdom, “every quote has a home”, “words worth keeping”, “the right words at the right moment”, “collect words, build wisdom”, “a living archive of human wisdom”, “a meaning layer”, “a nest of meaningful words”, “a tiny civilization of literary birds carrying human wisdom through time”.

**Words and vibes to avoid:**
snarky mascot energy, pushy gamification, “streak shame”, aggressive Duolingo threat humor, corporate flatness, generic quote-poster cliché, fake inspirational hustle-brand tone, militarized/tactical/dark masculine leadership visuals, “alpha warrior” aesthetics, real-person portraits in the Quipsly visual system, flattening cultures into costumes, homogenized diversity, “default beige Quipsly”, exact parody/caricature of protected characters or celebrities, “Pinterest quote board” shallowness, quote spam without source/context.

## 2. Visual language

### Overall aesthetic direction
The strongest visual direction is: **Cozy storybook + warm literary archive + soft app-native mascot world.**

**Visual references/inspirations:**
Duolingo-like mascot clarity (but gentler), storybook illustration, warm watercolor, parchment, old notebooks, library margins, tea stains, tiny lanterns, nests, quote scraps, ink, maps, pressed leaves, old books, cozy academia, animated margin doodles, paper-craft warmth, gentle whimsical UI.

**Anti-references:**
dark tactical leadership posters, realistic human portrait branding, corporate SaaS dashboards, neon dopamine cannon, military fetish aesthetics, generic motivational posters, overly realistic fantasy warrior imagery, big-eyed owl clone mascot, flat vector mascot without soul.

### Typography direction
- Display serif for “QuipLore” and quotable-person pages (warm literary/classic book typography)
- Friendly rounded sans such as Nunito, Inter, or similar for UI
- Hand-lettered/organic labels sparingly for Quipsly notes, margin labels, and cards

### Texture and material references
Use: parchment, cream paper, deckled paper edges, ink lines, watercolor washes, soft shadows, book cloth, leather-bound books, woven nests, twig textures, tea cups, lantern glow, pressed leaves, handwritten scraps, margin notes, old library cards.
*Avoid sterile flatness. The app can be modern, but the emotional material is analog.*

### Iconography style
Icons should be: soft outlined or gently filled, leaf/quill/nest/paper inspired, warm brown or muted green/gold, rounded, tactile, simple enough for mobile, not overly sharp or corporate.

### Illustration and photo treatment
**Major decision: No real human images in Quipsly/QuipLore visual language. Use Quipsly only.**
People, characters, historical figures, authors, and fictional figures should be represented by Quipsly inspired by their energy, wardrobe, setting, quote context, and symbolic motifs, not by realistic human portraits.
*Important caution:* avoid caricature, racial flattening, cultural costumes without care, or making one beige Quipsly in different outfits. Use respectful representation with meaningful diversity.

### Motion and animation ideas
- Quipsly hops onto saved quote cards
- Quipsly carries a quote scrap away
- Quipsly builds a nest from saved quotes
- loading state: Quipsly peeks over a card or “Loading ideas…”
- empty state: sleeping Quipsly in a blanket/nest
- personal library grows visually as a nest

### UI density, spacing, and shape language
UI should be: generous spacing, rounded cards, warm cream panels, soft shadows, tactile paper surfaces, cozy but not cluttered, mobile-first, card-driven, nested collections, readable typography.
Shape language: rounded rectangles, pill badges, parchment banners, quote cards, soft tabs, nests/circular cluster motifs.

## 3. Existing visual work

- **Mascot concept sheets:** small round sparrow-like mascot, satchel of quote scraps, building nests from paper, expressive poses. “Golden retriever librarian” energy. Avoid owl-drifting.
- **Icon explorations:** Classic Quipsly, Reader Quipsly, Nest Builder, Night Keeper, Simplified Mark, Messenger/Heartfelt Keeper.
- **Famous people / quote posters:** Quipsly inspired by famous people is delightful, but Quipsly should evoke the person or character, not impersonate or reproduce them exactly (avoid caricature).
- **Diversity and representation:** Avoid homogenizing all Quipsly. Representation should include coloration, feature variation, feather texture, clothing, accessories, posture, environments, and story, not just costumes.

**HighGroundOdyssey visuals:**
HighGroundOdyssey is Homer’s coaching page. It should also use Quipsly only (no people, no dark tactical warrior posters). Keep warm, grounded, encouraging coaching page feel using Quipsly as guides/navigators.

## 4. Product concepts explored

- **Quote Passport:** A user-facing collectible/progress system showing the user’s journey through quotes.
- **QuipStream / feed:** Vertically scrollable quote feed. Immersive one-at-a-time focus. Save, share, remix, nest, cite.
- **Lorelists / Nests:** Quote collections as playlists. Users can build sequences of quotes around a theme, mood, book, etc.
- **Quip cards / share cards:** Shareable quote cards that include quote, speaker, source, context, verification status, optional Quipsly illustration.
- **Source verification:** Central feature. QuipLore tracks exact quote text, canonical source, date, context, first known appearance, source evidence, confidence score, and disputed/misattributed notes.
- **Misquote/disputed quote handling:** Quote pages should support variants, paraphrases, uncertain sources, and commonly misquoted lineages.
- **User library / commonplace book:** Personal saved quotes should feel like a nest or modern commonplace book.

## 5. Quotable people pages
Dedicated pages for authors, leaders, artists, scientists, etc.
Should include:
- Quipsly-only illustration inspired by the person
- person identity metadata and short summary
- quote count and verification status
- topic/theme badges
- top verified quotes and quote cards with source/citation
- biography/context and works/sources list
- common misquotes/disputed attributions
- related nests/Lorelists

## 6. UX/UI components
- **Cards:** Quote Card, Quip Card/share card, Author/person card, Source/work card, Lorelist/Nest card, Verification card, Quipsly Note card.
- **Badges:** Verified, Disputed, Misattributed, Public Domain, Modern Copyright, Translation, Primary Source, Quote Lineage, Popular, Editor’s Pick, In Your Nest.
- **Quote pages:** A canonical and highly structured page (e.g., `/quote/<slug>-<id>`).
- **Feed interactions:** vertical swipe, save to nest, expand context, view source.
- **Mobile-first patterns:** thumb-friendly, bottom sheets, sticky search, tap to expand.

## 7. Open decisions
- **Naming:** QuipLore vs QuipsLore, Nests vs Lorelists.
- **Collective noun brainstorm:** a Nest, Library, Lore, Margin, Archive, Index, Cache, Corpus, or Branch of Quipsly.
- **Legal/copyright:** public domain quotes are safest; modern quotes may be copyrighted. Product must separate internal quote database vs publicly displayable exact quotes vs licensed content.
- **Visual production:** hand-drawn image asset pipeline vs SVG/component pipeline.

## 8. Build-ready summary
QuipLore is a warm, authoritative quote curation and discovery platform where meaningful words are collected, verified, contextualized, and connected. It should feel like a cozy literary archive powered by a serious quote/source engine. The emotional mascot/world is Quipsly, a species of adorable sparrow-like keeper-birds who collect quote scraps and build nests from words worth saving.

### Most important design commandments
1. Quipsly only, no real human portraits.
2. Warm and magical on the surface, rigorous underneath.
3. Quotes must be sourced, contextualized, and verified.
4. Do not homogenize Quipsly diversity.
5. Avoid dark tactical/military visual direction, even for HighGroundOdyssey.
6. Do not let feed mechanics become cheap dopamine sludge.
7. Make saving quotes feel like building a nest.
8. Make every quote page worthy of citation.
9. Use Quipsly as visual language, not decoration.
10. Keep the tone: golden retriever librarian, tiny keeper of meaning, emotionally sincere, curious, and cozy.
