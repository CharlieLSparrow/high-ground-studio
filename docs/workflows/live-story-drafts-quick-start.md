# Live Story Drafts Quick Start

## Who This Is For

This is for Homer, Chuck, and future Codex runs working on `Learning to Lead`.

The book is the thing. Homer wrote the book. Chuck is building the support layers around it: reflection, research bridges, pop-culture references, clips, and discussion. Live Story Drafts give that writing a home in the app without turning it into canonical manuscript truth too early.

## Where To Go

Open the internal Learning to Lead book page:

```text
/team/books/learning-to-lead
```

Use the `Story Map` view.

## What Story Map Is

Story Map is the internal planning view that starts from Homer's manuscript and breaks large book sections into reviewable story planning cards.

It is not a public page. It is not the manuscript. It is a practical place to see the book's story units before deciding what should become a real manuscript block or episode segment.

## What A Story Candidate Is

A Story Candidate is a planning card.

It says, "this part of Homer's book might be a story-sized unit." It can point to an opener, middle section, closer, handoff, source bin, or parked idea. A Story Candidate is useful for discussion, but it is not canonical manuscript truth.

## How To Create A Live Story Draft

1. Open `Story Map`.
2. Find the Story Candidate or chapter seed you want to work on.
3. Use the `Live Story Draft` panel on that card.
4. Add a draft title.
5. Write in `Draft Body`.
6. Choose the right status.
7. Add ordinary notes or Chuck Support Notes if needed.
8. Use `Create Live Draft`.

The draft is saved to the database. It does not edit `learning-to-lead.living.mdx`.

## How To Update A Draft

When a card already has a saved draft, the panel shows the latest draft first.

Edit the title, body, status, notes, or Chuck Support Notes, then choose `Save Latest Draft`.

That updates the draft currently shown in the panel.

## Save Latest Draft Versus Save As New Draft

Use `Save Latest Draft` when you are continuing the same draft.

Use `Save As New Draft` when you want to preserve the current version and create a separate draft attempt.

The newest updated draft is shown first on the Story Candidate card. Phase 1 does not have full revision history yet, so `Save As New Draft` is the practical way to keep separate versions.

## Statuses

`Rough`

Still being written.

`Needs Homer Review`

Homer should check voice, memory, story truth, comfort level, or what the story is really doing.

`Needs Chuck Review`

Chuck should shape the support layer, placement, research bridge, clip idea, or discussion path.

`Approved For Promotion`

The draft is approved as source material for a future manuscript edit. It is still not canonical manuscript truth.

`Parked`

Keep it saved, but do not use it in the current episode or manuscript pass.

`Promoted`

Reserved for a later controlled promotion workflow after a draft has become real manuscript truth. Phase 1 does not let the Story Map editor set this manually.

## Chuck Support Notes

Chuck Support Notes are for the layers around Homer's book:

- reflection prompts
- research bridge ideas
- clip or pop-culture references
- discussion questions
- source-review reminders
- concerns that need Homer review

They are not a place to smuggle unreviewed research into the manuscript. They are support notes for the later pass.

## Copy Promotion Packet

`Copy promotion packet` copies a handoff packet from the latest saved draft.

Use it when a draft is ready to become input for a separate manuscript promotion pass. The packet includes source context, the draft body, notes, support notes, and a checklist.

Copying the packet does not create a `ManuscriptBlock`. It does not edit files. It is a clean handoff for a later Codex/manual pass.

## What Does Not Happen Automatically

- The living manuscript does not change.
- No real `ManuscriptBlock` is created.
- Episode arrangements do not change.
- `virtual-splits.yml` does not change.
- Production YAML does not change.
- Public publish files do not change.
- Drafts do not become public pages.
- Research, clips, and production notes do not become verified claims.

## How This Replaces OneNote Over Time

OneNote can remain a backup, capture space, or source reference.

The primary writing home should move into the app over time because the app keeps the writing beside the Story Candidate, source block, episode context, status, and review notes. That makes the work easier for Homer, Chuck, and Codex to find without treating draft words as finished manuscript truth.

## Boundary Reminder

Live Story Drafts are database state.

They are saved writing in the app. They are not canonical manuscript truth. The canonical book remains `learning-to-lead.living.mdx` until a separate reviewed promotion pass changes it intentionally.
