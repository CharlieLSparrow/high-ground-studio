## Short Internal Summary

Episode 5 uses two Homer stories to explore trust as a practical leadership value.

Tip and the Winnemucca promise show how kept promises turn trust from an idea into proof. Milk Down the Drain shows how a leader can forgive a real mistake without pretending standards no longer matter. The Charlie reflections connect those stories to dependability, restored confidence, coaching, and removal only when required.

## Possible Public Description Draft

In this episode, Homer and Charlie explore why trust is built less by speeches than by kept promises, dependable follow-through, and honest standards.

The stories move from a lost dog in Winnemucca to a costly mistake on the dairy farm, asking a practical leadership question: how do we forgive mistakes without lowering the standard?

This draft description is not public-ready until Homer/Charlie approve the title, tone, references, and Episode 5/6 boundary.

## Key Themes

- trust as proof
- kept promises
- dependable follow-through
- forgiveness and accountability
- restored confidence
- standards that still matter
- coaching before removal
- values as behavior, not decoration

## Likely Audience Takeaway

Trust is not mainly a feeling people grant to leaders. It is evidence leaders accumulate through kept promises, steady follow-through, honest correction, and standards that are clear enough to protect both people and the work.
