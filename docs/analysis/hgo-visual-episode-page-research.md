# HGO Visual Episode Page Research

Date: 2026-05-21

This note focuses on the public projection system now being prototyped for
HighGroundOdyssey.com. The goal is not to imitate a podcast site. The goal is
to borrow useful patterns while avoiding the generic title/player/text-wall
episode page that makes HGO feel smaller than the Studio manuscript model.

## Research Boundary

- Internet inspection was available.
- Real HGO manuscript text was not used.
- Real HGO content was not copied into fixtures or pages.
- Several sites were only partially inspectable through search/open results,
  so those limitations are called out directly.

## Blunt Takeaways

- Most podcast pages are still archive pages with a player and a transcript.
  That is fine for mature media networks, but it is not enough for HGO.
- The best pages make the show's identity, audio state, show notes, transcript,
  and support paths easy to find.
- HGO has a better source model than most episode pages: Studio can provide
  structure, voice, quote state, handoff notes, and book relationships.
- HGO should use pages as projections from Studio, not as hand-authored blog
  posts.
- Staged previews matter because Homer needs to see work before public release.

## Examples Inspected

### Tim Ferriss Show

References:
- https://tim.blog/podcast/
- https://tim.blog/2018/09/20/all-transcripts-from-the-tim-ferriss-show/

Pattern:
- Large transcript library.
- Strong archive and resource orientation.
- Clear reuse/attribution caution for transcripts.

Borrow:
- Durable transcript/resource archive.
- Rights/reuse clarity around quoted material.

Avoid:
- Letting HGO become a high-function blog with a very long transcript library
  as its main public personality.

### The Dollop

References:
- Search inspection found official/listening surfaces and third-party transcript
  ecosystems, but the official site was not reliably inspectable here.
- Apple/listening pages expose source, tour, merch, and sponsor references.

Pattern:
- Fan and third-party transcript/search behavior exists because people want to
  navigate the archive.

Borrow:
- Archive search and source navigation are important for long-running shows.

Avoid:
- Depending on third-party transcript pages or leaving source material scattered.

### The Greatest Generation / Maximum Fun

Reference:
- https://maximumfun.org/podcasts/greatest-generation/

Pattern:
- Strong show landing page.
- Trailer/player near top.
- Latest episodes, archive filtering, people/host metadata, support/membership,
  platform links, and related show navigation.

Borrow:
- Clear show identity plus membership/support paths.
- People/role metadata.
- Archive filtering.

Avoid:
- Network archive sameness. HGO needs a more authored, story-forward page for
  each projection.

### Huberman Lab

Reference:
- https://www.hubermanlab.com/episode/how-smell-taste-and-pheromone-like-chemicals-control-you

Pattern:
- Episode type/date/title/topic.
- Platform links.
- Tabs/sections for show notes, timestamps, transcript, and behind the scenes.
- Resource links are central.

Borrow:
- Timestamp/beat navigation.
- Resource/source blocks that support claims.
- Episode pages as practical reference objects.

Avoid:
- Overloading HGO with protocol/resource density when the story needs to lead.

### 99% Invisible

Reference:
- Transcript/search inspection: https://99percentinvisible.org/episode/thermal-delight/transcript/

Pattern:
- Design/story brand with separate transcript surfaces.
- Transcript pages are accessible and indexable.

Borrow:
- Treat every episode as a designed object.
- Keep transcript/projection surfaces distinct when useful.

Avoid:
- Making the transcript the whole public experience.

### Darknet Diaries

Reference:
- https://darknetdiaries.com/episode/158/

Pattern:
- Episode number/title, topic tags, sponsors, attribution, transcript, and
  previous/next navigation.
- Artwork and show identity carry the mood.

Borrow:
- Source/attribution clarity.
- Visible episode metadata.
- Previous/next navigation that makes archive movement easy.

Avoid:
- Copying the dark mystery aesthetic. HGO needs its own leadership/book/story
  world.

### Acquired

Reference:
- https://www.acquired.fm/episodes/nvidia-the-dawn-of-the-ai-era

Pattern:
- Season/episode identity.
- Deep transcript.
- Links, sponsors, community calls, and companion material.

Borrow:
- Long-form episodes can become durable research objects.
- Season/series context matters.

Avoid:
- Opening with a transcript wall when the episode needs a visual promise.

### Hidden Brain

Reference:
- https://www.hiddenbrain.org/podcast/do-you-feel-invisible/

Pattern:
- Calm episode premise and illustration-led identity.
- Transcript availability through page controls and FAQ guidance.
- Thematic collection thinking.

Borrow:
- Calm, humane framing.
- Themed collection navigation.

Avoid:
- A public page that is only a descriptive essay plus transcript.

### Freakonomics

Reference:
- https://freakonomics.com/podcast/how-important-is-your-choice-of-words/

Pattern:
- Episode question/title.
- Transcript section is a major artifact.
- Show notes and transcript are search-visible.

Borrow:
- Strong central question.
- Transcript/search discoverability.

Avoid:
- SEO transcript conventions defining the whole design.

### Revisionist History

Reference:
- Direct current Pushkin episode inspection was limited by fetch errors; search
  results and transcript mirrors were available.

Pattern:
- The useful lesson is format-level: thesis, reversal, story tension, and
  narrative hook.

Borrow:
- Episode pages should have a sharp reason to exist.

Avoid:
- Treating a strong narrative show as a plain feed item.

## Direction For HGO

HGO should borrow the best parts:

- player state near the top,
- structured episode beats,
- transcript/source affordances,
- support/listen paths,
- previous/next movement,
- people/voice metadata,
- public-safe source state.

But HGO should add what these pages usually lack:

- staged previews before public release,
- a book/series map,
- a lifecycle model for synthetic, staged, live, and archived pages,
- a shared projection contract from Studio,
- filters for book-only, episode-only, both, internal, and public-safe work.

The current synthetic preview system is a low-risk way to prove that direction
without touching real HGO content.
