Attention is one of the most underrated forms of service.
 
Probably because from the outside it can look like nothing.  
No big speech.  
No visible heroics.  
No clever framework with a name that sounds expensive.  
Just a person staying with the work long enough to be useful.
 
But on the receiving end, real attention does not feel like nothing.  
It feels like respect, relief, and even like love.
 
I adore this story because it is so painfully specific.  
His one job wasn’t to interpret the moment, add side commentary or workshop his tight five.  
Just hold the light where the work was happening.
 
And honestly, how much of adulthood is just failing this assignment in more sophisticated ways?
 
How often am I “listening” while mostly waiting for my turn to talk?  
Or waving the flashlight around because some part of me would rather be interesting than useful?
 
I ask as a person whose natural habitat is side commentary.
 
There are whole books and bodies of research on listening, attunement, presence, co-regulation, all of it. In a 2020 set of studies on high-quality listening, people who felt genuinely listened to showed more self-insight, openness to change, and eve, lower prejudiced attitudes. Somebody paying real attention can literally make you less dug in.
 
So when you feel like a farm kid freezing beside a hole in the ground while dad is trying to fix the water.
 
Keep the beam where the work is.
 
That is a pretty good definition of maturity, actually.
