_Theme: Priorities ("Worst Lieutenant Ever")_  
[Chapter Ten: Part 2]  
I stopped. I let the shock cut me more than I should have. ”I’ve led over 200 aggressive combat missions and every one of my guys is still alive, how dare you, you punk who has never left the FOB; who has never lead anyone.” I only though it; didn’t say it. I couldn't think of any cleaver thing to say, like "I may be the worst Lieutenant you've ever heard of, but you have heard of me." instead I walked back, straight at him, my pointer finger raising and arm extending, then right passed him to the giant map on the wall behind him. I stabbed my finger onto the map board And loudly declared,. "Prepare to copy, grid, AB 1234 5678"  
"That's not the right grid," he protested "You made that up". I hadn't. I knew the map well. It was actually pretty close.  
"How do you know?" I retorted "Maybe I did make it up. Maybe all my reports are made up." I turned to leave again. "Look," I said in a slightly softer tone turning back slightly, ”I'm going to get some sleep. If you are going to be successful, you are going to have to figure out what's important." and I stepped through the tent flap pausing for a second to let my eyes adjust. Then disappeared into the quiet, lonely darkness.
