==By Sparrow==  
Nathan Young, by his own diagnosis, is an Alfa male of Alfa males. I watched once as a group of guys debated about where to eat lunch. Someone suggested Taco Bell for its proximity and convenience. The rest of the crowd, of 15 or so, all immediately scoffed him to shame, expressing their disdain for Taco Bell and describing that they would rather eat feces than be subjected to such toxic sludge. They bantered back and forth for probably 10 minutes figuring out where to go; long enough that they could have already ordered food somewhere and long enough that I was about to pick for them. Too expensive, too far, too crowded, too overrated. They finally settled on where to eat when Young walked in. "Where are we eating?" he asked the collective.  
"Burger King" a few chimed together, proud of their final consciences. The rest nodded in agreement.  
"Burger King?" Young made a face. "Ok, well, enjoy, I'm going to Taco Bell." With that he turned around and walked back out. Unaware of the Herculean deliberation it had taken to get to that verdict. They all looked at each other, shrugged, stood up, and all followed Young to Taco Bell.  
I held the title of Platoon Leader but it was clear to me who was in charge. So many times I put out directions and everyone looked to Young for approval, he nodded and they executed. Young was an E-5 "Buck" Sergeant, operating as a Squad leader because of a lack of other Non-Commissioned Officers in the Unit. When we mobilized I was told we could get backfilled if we requested additional E-6's. We received an E-6, Staff Sergeant, for our training at the National Training Center. I'm a little embarrassed to admit I don't remember a lot about him but he was not a good fit and was reassigned. In addition to referent power because of his charisma, Young was one of very few in the group who had deployed before. His knowledge of deployment gave him unmatched expert power that he handled professionally.  
The remarkable characteristic of SGT Young was not the power he wielded but the power he willingly gave up for the good of the of the order. George Washington is known for his time as the General of the Revolution, President of the Constitutional Convention, and first President of the United States of America but he is remembered and revered for ceding his post for the good of the Nation. So many times I heard SGT Young say, "L-T's in charge, so we listen to him". As a self-conscious, inexperienced kid from the sticks, I would not be where I am today without the power of SGT Young as the first follower.  
==First follower==  
But the impact goes far beyond talking others into playing follow the leader.  
We arrived in Kuwait only to a barrage of more training. By 2006, no one wanted the liability of sending a unit into battle without "sufficient" training. By the time we finished, we joked that all the training had been a torturous ploy to make us beg to be done with training so we could finally relax and go fight a war. Training complete, and finally waiting for our flight into Iraq the next day, I met with the company leadership for an update. "There are a couple of late deployers who completed individual training at Fort Bliss and are joining us as augmentees." the first Sergeant announced. "There's a Staff Sergeant with them. Lieutenant Sparrow, you have first pick in the whole battalion since Second Platoon is short a squad leader."  
"We're not short a squad leader" I retorted.  
"SGT Young's not an E-6". First Sergeant reminded me.  
"SGT Young is a great squad leader." I came back.  
"Ok, well, you are short an E-6. So he's yours if you want him." the First Sergeant nearly insisted .  
"But we don't want him." I was clear.  
"You should take him," Lieutenant Peterson jumped in with much older and wiser wisdom. "At least think about it."  
"I don't need to think about it." I fired back  
"At least go talk to your platoon." he pleaded.  
"Ok, I'll go talk to the Squad leaders and be right back to let you know we don't need him."
 
"Who is it?" SGT Young asked at the news.  
"I don't know, I never even met him." I said, surprised at the question, I looked at the piece of paper in my hand for the first time. "Staff Sergeant…. Head-le-sten" I pronounced.  
"Head?" Young inquired a little excited. I just looked at him "Is it James Heddlesten?" he asked.  
"Uhh.." I looked at the paper again "I don't know"  
"If it's James Heddlesten, then we want him." SGT Young's immediate response shocked me.  
"He would take over your squad." I asserted, to be clear what was at stake. I looked at SSG Brummett and SSG Hale to talk sense into him.  
"It's his squad to give up." Hale responded with indifference. Brummett agreed. I was confused. That was not the way I thought that would go. Months we had trained together. Really, an entire year Brummett, Young, and I spent preparing for this day and here we were, at the starting gate, and some stranger was about to waltz in and take over Young's squad that he trained, and baby sat, and loved. But, like Hale said, it was his squad to give up and we trusted Young. So, I went back to the First Sergeant who confirmed that it was in-fact James, and just like that, Heddlesten joined the team.
