To be clear at the outset of this writing; if you are not a Soldier from 2nd Platoon, B Company, 321st Engineer Battalion, who served in Ramadi, Iraq, from 2006-2007, or their immediate families, this book is not written for you or with you in mind. We hope you read it. Please read it. We want you to know our story. But It is not written to appeal to a mass audience, or a military audience, or _any_ audience. We wrote it for us; to tell our story, form our perspective, and the perspective of those on the team . We recognize these are only a few stories from one narrow perspective. We acknowledge that perspectives shift and are skewed by the passage of time coupled by the experiences of life. We tell this story now because the wounds have scared enough to feel less misunderstood. We felt angry for a long time. We even reminded ourselves to stay angry, wanting to feel some emotion as a tribute to the dead and to the wounded. We have worried that, if somehow we stopped being angry we would forget and thus dishonor our comrades. But no matter how we felt then or how we feel now we can never fully repay, but will forever seek to honor them.  
We expect no praise. We accept no sympathy. We request only that you remember the fallen, the wounded, those who sacrificed, and those who risked.  
Help us remember, lest we forget.
