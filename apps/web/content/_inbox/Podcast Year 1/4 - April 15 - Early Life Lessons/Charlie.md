I was a pretty awkward kid (hold for gasps from the audience). I know I know, I'm a pretty awkward adult too. But in spite of the struggles and anxiety it has caused in my life at times, on the whole I'm grateful for the lessons I've learned from being awkward and self-conscious. The biggest one has been that no matter how weird I feel in a given situation, if I shift the focus from myself to others it always goes better. Once I'm focused on the person in front of me, and trying to understand them, I almost always like them. Once it's clear to them that I genuinely care about them and what they're saying something magic happens, they lean in.
 
Like all of our lessons in this chapter this idea is embarrassingly simple, if you pay attention to the person in front of you instead of yourself, you'll begin to understand them, if you understand them you'll probably care about them, and then treat them the way they want to be treated.
 
As simple as all of that is, are you doing it? As often as you should?
 
Me neither, let's work on it together.
 
In _The Charisma Myth_, Olivia Fox Caban breaks down the research around charisma to three things Power, Presence, and Warmth. If you're with someone, pay attention to them, be present with them, try to understand them, as you understand them you'll care more about them creating warmth. All of this pulls you outside of yourself making it easier to find confidence and power.
 
This is obviously way over simplified, but simple triggers leading you through to healthier mindsets is what it's all about.
 
So being kind isn't just a nice thing to do, it's critical to life, work, and even to your internal world. It's worth perusing and remembering to do for its own merits, and for the good that it leads you to.
