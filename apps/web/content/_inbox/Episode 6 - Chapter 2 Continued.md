In a 1995 Academy of Management Review article that's become a cornerstone in trust research, Roger Mayer, James Davis, and David Schoorman argued that people tend to judge trustworthiness through three things: ability, benevolence, and integrity. Can you do the job, do you mean well, and do your actions keep pace with your promises. You can be passionate and liked without being trusted.
