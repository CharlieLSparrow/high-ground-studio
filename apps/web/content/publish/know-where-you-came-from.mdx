This episode turns toward ancestry and inheritance: the stories, people, and family legends that shaped the ground beneath Scott's life long before he was old enough to name what they had given him.

## Scott's Book Excerpt

The second "to do" in this book — the first being to look for the extraordinary in the ordinary and tell your own story — is this: **find out who you are and where you came from.**

Knowing who you are establishes a starting point. It provides a lens through which to look into the future and decide where you want to be. I would only add this: understanding where you are begins with knowing where you came from.

Your backstory — even your setbacks — becomes part of the setup for who you can become.

That is part of why the family story of Chub and Jack matters. They worked together, gave the task all they had, and became a lasting picture of trust, loyalty, effort, and pulling through.

The invitation is not to become impressed with my family lore. The invitation is to find out where **you** came from, who **you** are, and where **you** are going.
